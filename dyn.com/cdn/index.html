Leveraging CDNs

Organizations that have invested in a CDN to accelerate their web performance understand the impact that faster page loads have on their bottom line. By protecting this investment with a dedicated DNS solution, these organizations ensure traffic connects to this CDN as quickly and reliably as possible.

Get The Most Out of Your Content Delivery Network

Key Elements

  • ★ Content Delivery Networks: More often referred to as "CDNs," Content Delivery Networks are an organization of distributed servers that deliver content to visitors of your website.
  • ★ Redundancy At All Levels: Eliminate single points of failure by incorporating your CDN into your overall web infrastructure. Mitigate issues with any component by routing traffic to healthy endpoints.
  • ★ Establish Your Rules: Define the Traffic Management rules that best fit your customer needs, business rules, and business continuity planning.
  • ★ Endpoint Agnostic: Treat all elements of your infrastructure equally, leveraging your CDN, data centers, and other cloud services to deliver the right content from the best location every time.
  • ★ Multi-CDN: Avoid getting locked into a single CDN provider. Dyn Traffic Management makes load-balancing between competing CDNs a breeze. Save money while improving performance and reliability. When CDNs compete, you win!

A CDN is a globally distributed set of servers that delivers your content to visitors of your web applications from the optimal location available for each visitor, ensuring higher availability and improved performance. While CDNs can dramatically improve the speed of your page loads, it’s only one piece of the performance equation.

Web pages are a collection of objects that all get downloaded whenever somebody visits the page. Some of these objects are best served from your CDN, while others may be loaded from your own data centers or other cloud services. With dozens of these objects requiring DNS lookups, there’s potential for delays in page loads and unreachable content if your DNS provider isn’t able to quickly route each request to a healthy endpoint.

Combining your CDN with a Managed DNS solution like Dyn’s Traffic Management protects your brand and improves your customers’ experience on your websites. Traffic Management provides the reliability, performance, and flexibility to protect against outages within your CDN, deliver customers to your websites at unmatched speeds, and shape your traffic to best utilize each part of your web infrastructure.

Empower Your CDN to Deliver For Your Business

Customer Experience

One of the most important parts of your customers' experience is the initial impression they get the first time they access your site. While your CDN reduces page load times once somebody connects to your site, your DNS platform dictates how long it takes them to connect. To make that first impression a lasting one, combine your CDN with Dyn's Traffic Management to achieve unmatched performance.

Redundancy

Part of creating a business continuity plan is ensuring that you have redundancy across all of your core systems. Dyn Traffic Management makes it easy to ensure there’s an endpoint ready to take over in case your CDN experiences an outage or any other part of your infrastructure goes down. Removing all single points of failure ensures traffic will continue flowing to your websites at all times, day and night.

Flexibility

An endpoint agnostic DNS platform offers the flexibility to route your traffic to the optimal endpoints in each region of the world. Lower your total cost of ownership by employing different CDNs in certain regions or spreading your traffic volume across multiple providers. Traffic Management simplifies adding or switching CDN and cloud services, giving you the freedom to evolve your infrastructure as your business grows.
